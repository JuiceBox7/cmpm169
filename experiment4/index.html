Experiment 4 - Images, Video, & Sound Art

Imitate

For this assignment, I referenced the idea of using video for our experiment from class, and also a piece from Generative Design. The piece from Generative Design really captivated me, as it took an image and warped it in a way that resembled a wet canvas. I really wanted to use this idea and mesh it using video, and decided this was best done by taking a frame from the video feed and warping that frame. From the piece I referenced, I would mainly need a block of code that did the warping effect on the image.

Integrate

For integration, I added the code block I mentioned above, as well as put in the lines of code that are needed to capture video in p5js. Since the warping just warped whatever was on the canvas, be it image or text, it was very easy to mesh these two ideas together. In the art that I referenced, they used an image they had saved, but in my case I just replaced the image with video. I changed a few things from the warping code, but for the most part kept things in tact. Essentially I changed a few values. There wasn't a lot of time or steps spent on integration, as most of my time was spent in figuring out what to imitate and how to innovate on that.

Innovate

For this step, I spent a lot of time trying to figure out how to best utilize the video effect with the warping effect. Since the draw() function gets called every frame, a new image is being drawn over the old one, which essentially covers up all the warping that is done in that same frame. This creates a sort of “glitchy” camera feel which is cool, but loses the warped “watery” feel I enjoyed about the piece I found and used as inspiration. After a lot of thinking and trying a few ideas out, one being that a frame is taken and warped every 10 seconds, I came to the conclusion that the warping effect does need quite a bit of time and it would be difficult to do with live video. However, it did lead me to what would be my final design. I made it so that there would be a countdown that would begin on clicking the container, and at the end of the container the first frame supplied from the camera will be used as the photo that gets warped.
