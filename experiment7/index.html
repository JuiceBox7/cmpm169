Experiment 7 - Data Visualization & Networks

Imitate

For this assignment, I referenced a piece I found online. I was very lost in what to do for this experiment and it took me a long time to figure out what to do. I couldn't find a lot of good references on most websites, but found this off of Google and really liked the concept and design. Part of why it stood out to me was the fact that it wasn't using any kind of real data, which was one of my main concerns for this experiment. I didn't have any ideas of any data that I could use for this assignment, but the idea of using fictional information in this way was very appealing.

Integrate

For the integration step, there wasn't too much that needed to be done in order to integrate it with my idea, since my idea would basically be taking this concept and expanding upon it, with my own unique information and planet designs. However, due to how I did this experiment, my integration step and innovation step are closely related in what I did for each of them. I had to get my own images for the planets, as well as make my own csv file, for easier integration. Most of my work came from creating this csv file, as well as adding some fields for additional information, which I'll talk about more under innovation. I also had to change the size of the rect used as the background for the planet's information, to account for my additional fields.

Innovate

For innovation, I added some fields as I mentioned earlier. With the way that it was coded beforehand, I had to be extremely particular about the mouse offset, to ensure that the text looked in uniform with the rest of the text that already existed. A lot of my time was spent creating the csv file, which I decided to do within Visual Studio Code, despite the process maybe being much more simple through Excel or Google Sheets. I wrote up to 49 unique planet descriptions, and copy and pasted the results up to 197 lines, meaning that the user has exactly 196 functioning clicks until they run out of planets and need to reload the page. By prior design the planet image that is used for each planet is random, which was a nice touch.
